Immigration Before 1790

In order to talk about immigration to the country we now know as the United States it is important to trace back the first immigrant group which would become the ancestors to many Native American cultures. It is believed that around 20k years ago the first humans stepped in America and from them stemmed the native tribes we know today and through history. In the year 1000, Vikings would arrive to this foreign land and thousands of years later European explorers would start the great European migration. Political and religious freedom was enough of a drive to put these different European groups on a 3 month sail on the roughest conditions.

But Europeans were not the only group to migrate, black slaves were brought from West Africa. Servitude became a large part of American History and one that has left a lasting scar on the country. Slaves were also brought from the Caribbean.
